# FleetPulse – In-Depth Prompts for Google AI Image Generator

Use these in Google’s image AI (e.g. Imagen / Gemini image generation) to create **banner** and **logo** assets for FleetPulse. The current favicon is staying as-is; this document focuses only on hero/header imagery and the main logo. Because there’s no strict character limit, prompts are written to be as descriptive and precise as possible so the model has full context and can produce on-brand, professional results.

---

## Brand context (for your reference)

- **Product:** FleetPulse – a modern fleet management platform (B2B SaaS).
- **What it does:** Track vehicles, mileage, oil changes, maintenance, drivers, inspections, documents, and fleet health.
- **Audience:** Fleet managers, operations teams, companies that run trucks/vehicles.
- **Vibe:** Professional, trustworthy, modern, clean, premium but not flashy. Indigo and purple are the primary brand colors (gradients from deep indigo through violet to purple work well).
- **Avoid:** Literal photos of trucks, clip-art cars, cheesy stock imagery, playful or cartoonish style, cluttered compositions, any text unless you specifically want a wordmark.

## Banner (hero / header)

**Purpose:** Full-width imagery for the landing hero, dashboard header, or marketing pages. Content will be overlaid (headline, subhead, CTA), so the image should have **no text and no logos**, with enough contrast and calm areas for white or light text to sit comfortably. Aim for **16:9 or wider** (e.g. 3:1) so it crops well on desktop and mobile.

## Tips for best results

- **Banners:** Always specify “no text, no logos” so you can overlay your own headline and CTA. Mention “areas for overlay text” or “calm areas” if you want space for copy. Stating “16:9” or “wide format” helps with composition.
- **Logos:** If the model struggles with readable text, generate the **icon only** and add “FleetPulse” yourself in Figma, CSS, or your design tool using a font you already use (e.g. Inter, Plus Jakarta Sans).
- **Colors:** Being specific helps. You can say “indigo and purple” or reference hex-like tones (#4f46e5, #7c3aed) if the tool accepts them. “Gradient from indigo to purple” usually gets close to the FleetPulse brand.
- **Quality and style:** Phrases like “high quality,” “minimal,” “professional,” “flat design,” “suitable for B2B,” and “no clip art” tend to improve output. For logos, “works on light and dark backgrounds” and “recognizable at small sizes” are useful.
- **Iteration:** If a result is too busy, add “more minimal” or “more negative space.” If colors are off, try “deeper indigo” or “more purple.” You can also paste one of the longer prompts and then append a short tweak (e.g. “Make the gradient darker” or “Softer, more blur”).
- **Aspect ratio:** For banners, explicitly ask for “16:9” or “wide banner.” For logo marks, ask for “square,” “1:1,” or “square format.”
